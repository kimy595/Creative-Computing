Assignment 2

I interviewed my uncle, Joongdo Kim for this assignment.
He is a semiconductor researcher and developer at Samsung.
I chose to interview him because he was the only expert that I known of,
and I thought it would be interesting to learn about something that I had no knowledge before.

Me: Hi, Thank you for participating this interview.
JD : No problem
Me: So, please explain about semiconductor. What is semiconductor?
JD : Well, before we think of what semiconductor is, I will explain what is conductor and non-conductor first.
Conductor is a material that flows well with electricity or heat, such as iron, wire, aluminum, and gold.
Conversely, non-conductor is a material that does not flow electricity or heat such as glass, ceramics, and plastic.
In electrical engineering, the degree of electricity flow is called "electrical conductivity".
Conductors have a very high electrical conductivity, while insulators are almost zero.
Then, what do you think semiconductor would be??
Me: Um... a material that's in somewhere between two..? Or combined.
JD : Quite right! It's a substance with intermediate propertie between conductor and non-conductor(insulator).
Me: Okay. So what's the significancy of semiconductor? What's its main role?
JD : It acts as the brain of the machine. That is, it functions to store,calculate,infer and judge everything.
It plays a key role in all electronic and mechanical products.
The competent semiconductor has the merit that it is easy to control the property of material according to what kind of artificial manipulation is done.
Me: So it bascially takes the ledership of the signals in products.
JD : Correct.
Me: What do you mean by the artificial manipulation??
JD : Pure semiconductor almost dosen't electrify such as non-conductor.
But when it's subjected to any kind of artificial manipulation, such as application of light or heat, injection of certain impurity, electricity flows like conductor.
Conductors are well-run but difficult to control.
However, semiconductors have the advantage of easy control
of the properties of materials depending on what kind of artificial manipulation is applied.
Me: Wow. I guess it has great potential inherented. Then how does its speed determined?
JD : Um, it's quite complicated. The main thing is to optimize the semiconductor's circuit design.
Me: What do you mean by optimizing?
JD : When there are high number of electron that's responsible for the speed, the speed increases.
Which we say as'the charge density becomes high'. Then, as the mobility of the charge gets higher,
the semiconductor's speed gets faster.
There are so many factors that affect the density and mobility of this charge that I can't even explain right now,
but the most important thing is that you have to design a semiconductor circuit well.
When making a semiconductor circuit, it's important to make them not interfere with each other.
One of the material we use is called low Dk.
Me: What is the status of future semiconductor industry that you are looking forward to?
JD : Being the brain of every electronic and mechanical product is itself the greatest value of semiconductors.
So it will be an integral part of artificial intelligence, robots, autonomous vehicles, and supercomputing.
This means it has intimite relationship with Nanotechnology.
It's a essential technology to make semiconductor smaller, faster, and outstanding.
Me: Howcome?
JD : Future devices shrink. As they become smaller, semiconductor also shrinks.
Nowadays, tecnology is so developed that the the distance between the circuit and the circuit that it's less than a mm.
We could say semiconductor is typical instance of nanotechnology.
Me: Wow that's another really interesting part! I feel like I've learned something that's very scientific for the first time in a while.
JD : Haha.
Me: Thank you so much for the interview. It's been a great help.
